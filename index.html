About

Hi my name is Alejandro! I am very interested in the interface between tangible circuits and programming that can be used to solve real world problems and help people.

I just finished my undergraduate degree in 6-2 (Electrical Engineering and Computer Science) at MIT. I will be continuing to a Masters of Engineering with Luis Velasques workking on mass spectrometetry in cube satellites to measure the ionosphere in space. I like combining cool ideas and bringing projects to life with my passion for the environment and sustainability.

I am invested in the MIT Outdoors Club at MIT as well as several Sustainability clubs and initiatives including my role as a student representative to the CAAC(Climate Action Advisory Comittee)—composed of students, faculty, and staff. Furthermore, I have recently worked on research creating an evaporative vegetable cooler for use in low-income communities in Kenya. This work is done with Eric Verploegen in the MIT D-Lab.

When not in the lab, I can be found in a random yoga position, 20ft high on a wall climbing, 10 miles away running, eating all the food in front of me, or maybe working...
